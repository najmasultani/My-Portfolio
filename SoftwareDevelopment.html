Software Development Projects

As an engineering science student at UofT Engineering, I have had the privilege of working on three captivating design projects. Through these experiences, I have been able to demonstrate my expertise, foster creativity, and gain valuable insights into the world of innovative product design.

The projects I have worked on include:

Praxis II - Blind Curling Training Device

The Praxis II project was an exciting opportunity for me to work on a design solution that aimed to empower blind individuals in participating in the sport of curling. Our objective was to create a device that would enable them to learn and practice curling independently, without the need for a sighted guide.

Our innovative design consisted of several key components. We implemented an overhead camera to capture images of the curling sheet and stones. Using advanced OpenCV software, we developed a stone detection system that analyzed the images and determined the precise locations of the stones. To provide real-time feedback, we incorporated an audio output system that conveyed the stone locations to the blind curlers through calibrated loudspeakers. Additionally, we designed a user-friendly tactile map remote control that allowed blind individuals to select their targets and mentally visualize the entire curling field.

Working on the Praxis II project enabled me to apply my creativity, problem-solving skills, and engineering design expertise. I embraced the iterative design process, from brainstorming and prototyping to testing and refining the final product. This project ignited my passion for inclusive design and reinforced my commitment to leveraging technology to make a positive impact in people's lives.
